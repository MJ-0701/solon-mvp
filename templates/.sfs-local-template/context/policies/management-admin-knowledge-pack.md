# Management/Admin Knowledge Pack Inventory

This file is a compact filled guidance pack for Management/Admin work. Use it
when a sprint, plan, review, or release touches money movement, bookkeeping,
tax, accounting, contracts, payroll/contractors, receipts, invoices, budgets,
or business compliance. Apply only the matching ids.

This pack is not professional legal, tax, or accounting advice. Do not invent
jurisdiction-specific thresholds, filing rules, or tax interpretations. When a
decision affects filings, payroll, tax exposure, regulated reporting, or
material financial statements, surface the need for a qualified accountant,
tax advisor, or lawyer.

## Activation Rules

- Activate only when the work changes financial records, money flow, reporting,
  compliance exposure, or evidence needed for future finance/admin decisions.
- A pre-revenue experiment needs a light ledger and receipt habit, not a full
  ERP process.
- First revenue, recurring billing, refunds, payroll/contractors, tax filings,
  cross-border sales, or investor/lender reporting increase admin depth.
- Keep management/admin separate from Strategy/PM: Strategy/PM asks whether the
  business decision is right; Management/Admin asks whether the records,
  controls, cash view, and compliance evidence are ready.

## ADM-SCALE - Review Depth By Business Stage

- ADM-SCALE-001: A throwaway experiment needs a spend cap, receipt capture, and stop condition.
- ADM-SCALE-002: A pre-revenue product needs expense categories, cash runway view, and vendor/subscription list.
- ADM-SCALE-003: First paid customer needs invoice/receipt evidence, payment status, refund policy, and bookkeeping category.
- ADM-SCALE-004: Recurring revenue needs monthly close habit, accounts receivable/payable view, revenue/refund handling, and churn/cash reconciliation.
- ADM-SCALE-005: Contractor, payroll, or commission work needs role, contract, payment schedule, tax-document owner, and approval evidence.
- ADM-SCALE-006: Tax or statutory filing work needs jurisdiction, entity type, period, source documents, filing owner, due date, and advisor checkpoint.
- ADM-SCALE-007: Cross-border, multi-currency, grants, investor, or lender reporting needs currency treatment, withholding/VAT/GST/sales-tax flag, covenant/reporting requirement, and specialist review.

## ADM-PROP - Proposition Inventory

- ADM-PROP-001: Cash runway must state current cash, expected inflow, committed outflow, burn, and decision horizon.
- ADM-PROP-002: Bookkeeping needs one source of truth for income, expense, receipt, invoice, refund, and transfer records.
- ADM-PROP-003: Personal and business money should be separated in accounts, cards, receipts, and reimbursement notes.
- ADM-PROP-004: Every expense should carry date, vendor, amount, currency, category, purpose, payment method, and receipt/source evidence.
- ADM-PROP-005: Invoice numbers, customer names, issue dates, due dates, payment status, and refund/credit notes must be traceable.
- ADM-PROP-006: Tax-sensitive items must be flagged without guessing the final tax treatment.
- ADM-PROP-007: Payroll, contractor, and commission payments need agreement, rate, period, approval, payment evidence, and local tax-document owner.
- ADM-PROP-008: Subscription and vendor spend needs owner, renewal date, cancellation path, data-risk note, and cost justification.
- ADM-PROP-009: Monthly close should reconcile bank/payment-provider balances to the ledger and list unresolved differences.
- ADM-PROP-010: Pricing, discount, refund, and credit policies should name finance impact before they become user promises.
- ADM-PROP-011: Financial exports and reports must avoid leaking secrets, bank data, tax IDs, or personal data into AI prompts or logs.
- ADM-PROP-012: Admin decisions need audit-friendly evidence when they affect filings, cash commitments, contracts, payroll, or investor/lender trust.
- ADM-PROP-013: A solo founder still needs a minimal internal control: maker/checker pause before filings, large payments, payroll, or irreversible account changes.
- ADM-PROP-014: Legal/tax/accounting uncertainty should be recorded as a question with owner and due date, not resolved by AI confidence.

## ADM-FILL - Operating Guidance

### ADM-FILL-CASH - Cash And Runway

- Keep a compact cash view: current balance, committed expenses, expected
  income, runway, and the next decision date. The goal is decision clarity, not
  accounting theater.
- Separate one-time setup spend from recurring burn. Recurring burn changes the
  survival curve faster than a single tool purchase.
- For paid products, connect product metrics to cash reality: sales, refunds,
  payment delays, fees, churn, and support cost.

### ADM-FILL-BOOKS - Bookkeeping And Evidence

- Capture receipts and invoices when the event happens. Future cleanup is more
  expensive and less reliable.
- Use stable categories that match how the founder decides, then map to the
  accountant's chart of accounts when professional reporting is needed.
- Keep source evidence close to the transaction: receipt, invoice, contract,
  bank/payment-provider line, approval note, and refund/credit note.

### ADM-FILL-TAX - Tax And Compliance Boundary

- Before making tax statements, identify jurisdiction, entity type, tax period,
  transaction type, counterparty location, and source documents.
- Do not guess tax rates, filing thresholds, deductibility, employment status,
  or revenue recognition rules. Surface them as advisor questions.
- Track due dates and owner explicitly. A tax/compliance task without owner and
  date is a future incident, even if the document looks complete.

### ADM-FILL-CONTROL - Solo-Founder Controls

- One-person operations still need friction before high-impact actions: large
  payment, payroll, filing, bank/account setting, production billing change, or
  contract signature.
- Keep an audit trail for who decided, what evidence was used, what was
  deferred, and when it should be revisited.
- Mask or avoid sensitive financial data before using AI. Use structure,
  categories, and synthetic examples when the exact value is not required.

## ADM-REVIEW - Review Questions

- Is the financial/admin impact visible, or did the work only discuss product
  behavior?
- Are money movement, invoices, refunds, expenses, and subscriptions traceable
  to source evidence?
- Are tax/accounting/legal uncertainties surfaced as questions instead of AI
  conclusions?
- Does the task have the right depth for the business stage, without forcing a
  heavy process onto a small experiment?
- Are sensitive financial identifiers, bank details, tax IDs, and personal data
  kept out of prompts, logs, and public artifacts?

## ADM-EVIDENCE - Suggested Evidence

- Cash/runway snapshot with date and assumption note.
- Ledger or transaction export with categories and receipt/source links.
- Invoice/refund/payment status list.
- Monthly reconciliation note: bank/payment-provider balance versus ledger.
- Tax/accounting advisor question list with owner, due date, and source docs.
- Approval/decision record for large payments, payroll/contractor payments,
  filings, contracts, pricing, discounts, refunds, or investor/lender reports.
